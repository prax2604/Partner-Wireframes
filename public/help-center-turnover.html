Turnover Commission Model Explained

Turnover model overview

The Turnover commission model provides a fixed commission for every lot or contract traded by your referred clients, regardless of whether those trades are profitable or result in losses. This model offers consistent, predictable income based on trading volume.

Key points

With the Turnover model, you earn commissions based on the volume of trades your clients make, not their trading results. Commission rates vary by product type and are paid on both winning and losing trades.

This model is ideal for partners who refer clients who trade frequently and is applicable to all Deriv products, including both options and CFDs (although CFD commissions follow a different structure through the IB program).

How turnover works

When your referred clients place trades, you earn a commission based on the volume traded, with rates varying by product type. The commission structure for the Turnover plan is product-specific:

  • Digital Options: Based on the payout probability of the client's contracts
  • Multipliers: 40% of client fee
  • Accumulator Options: 40% of client fee
  • Turbo Options: 40% of client fee
  • Vanilla Options: 40% of client fee
  • Lookbacks (only on SmartTrader): 0.8% of the trade stake

Important note:

Due to regulatory restrictions, EU-based affiliates can sign up for the Turnover plan but can only refer clients who reside outside the EU.

Digital Options commissions

For Digital Options, the commission rate depends on the payout probability of the client's contract. Here's how it works:

Payout Probability Commission Rate
0% - 20% 0.4%
20% - 50% 0.5%
50% - 80% 0.7%
80% - 95% 1.0%

Example calculation for Digital Options

1

Your client trades Digital Options with a stake of $10

2

The payout probability is 40%, which falls in the 20%-50% range (commission rate = 0.5%)

3

Commission calculation: $10 √ó 0.5% = $0.05

Benefits of turnover model

The Turnover model offers several distinct advantages:

1. Predictable income

Earn commissions regardless of client profitability, creating more stable and predictable revenue streams.

2. Available for all products

Unlike Revenue Share, the Turnover model applies to all trading products, including both options and CFDs.

3. Rewards client activity

The more your clients trade, the more you earn, directly rewarding your efforts to refer active traders.

4. No dependency on client results

Your earnings aren't affected by whether your clients win or lose, removing that uncertainty from your income projections.

Comparison with other models

Different commission models suit different partner strategies and client types:

Turnover vs. Revenue Share

  • Turnover: Based on trading volume regardless of outcomes; more stable earnings.
  • Revenue Share: Based on client net losses; potentially higher earnings with losing clients.

Turnover vs. CPA

  • Turnover: Ongoing commissions based on trading volume; unlimited earning potential.
  • CPA: One-time payment for client acquisition; simpler but limited earning potential.
