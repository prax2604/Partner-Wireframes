CPA Commission Model

The CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) model is a straightforward commission structure that rewards you with a fixed payment for each qualified client you refer to Deriv. This client-focused approach offers immediate, predictable earnings without waiting for trading activity, making it ideal for partners who excel at client acquisition.

Important: CPA offers immediate returns as soon as a client meets the qualification criteria, regardless of their future trading activity or profitability.

Key Benefits of CPA

Immediate Rewards

Earn fixed payments as soon as referred clients meet the qualification criteria, without waiting for them to generate trading volume or revenue.

Predictable Earnings

Know exactly how much you'll earn for each qualified client, making it easier to forecast your commission income and plan your marketing efforts.

Region-Based Rates

Benefit from market-specific CPA rates that reflect the value of clients from different geographic regions, allowing for targeted promotion.

How CPA Works

The CPA model focuses solely on client acquisition and initial engagement. Here's a simplified explanation of how it works:

  1. You refer potential clients to Deriv through your affiliate links
  2. Clients register and make a qualifying deposit
  3. Clients complete the qualification criteria (typically making a minimum number of trades)
  4. You receive a fixed CPA payment for each qualified client
Note: CPA payments are one-time only, meaning you earn for each client's initial qualification but not for their ongoing activity. For clients with high trading potential, Revenue Share or Turnover Commission might offer higher long-term earnings.

Qualification Criteria

To earn a CPA commission, your referred clients must meet specific requirements:

Requirement Details
Account Verification Client must complete the account verification process
Minimum Deposit Client must deposit at least $100 or equivalent in their account
Trading Activity Client must place at least 5 trades with a minimum total stake of $100
Qualification Window All criteria must be met within 30 days of registration
Pro Tip: To maximize CPA conversions, focus on attracting clients who are genuinely interested in trading and guide them through the qualification process with tutorials or support.

Calculation Examples

Example 1: European Market Focus

Scenario: You refer 15 clients from Europe, and 10 of them meet all qualification criteria within the specified timeframe.

Calculation:

  • Qualified clients: 10
  • CPA rate for Europe: $200 per client
  • 10 clients × $200 = $2,000

Your commission would be $2,000.

Example 2: Multi-region Campaign

Scenario: You run a global campaign and refer qualified clients from different regions: 5 from Europe, 8 from Asia-Pacific, and 4 from Latin America.

Calculation:

  • Europe: 5 clients × $200 = $1,000
  • Asia-Pacific: 8 clients × $150 = $1,200
  • Latin America: 4 clients × $120 = $480
  • Total: $1,000 + $1,200 + $480 = $2,680

Your commission would be $2,680.

Example 3: Performance-Based Rate Increase

Scenario: After consistently referring high-quality clients, your account manager increases your Europe CPA rate to $250. You then refer 12 qualified European clients.

Calculation:

  • Qualified clients: 12
  • Enhanced CPA rate for Europe: $250 per client
  • 12 clients × $250 = $3,000

Your commission would be $3,000.

Comparing Commission Models

Different commission models suit different partner strategies and client types:

CPA vs. Revenue Share

  • CPA: One-time fixed payment per qualified client; immediate returns but limited to initial acquisition.
  • Revenue Share: Ongoing percentage of revenue generated by clients; potentially higher long-term earnings for active traders.

CPA vs. Turnover Commission

  • CPA: Fixed payment regardless of trading volume; simpler to track and predict.
  • Turnover: Ongoing commission based on trading volume; unlimited earning potential for clients who trade frequently.
Which model should you choose? CPA is ideal if you excel at client acquisition but don't want to depend on their trading activity. If your clients typically trade actively over long periods, Revenue Share or Turnover Commission might offer higher total earnings.
