Commission Models Explained

When you join Deriv Partners, you'll need to choose a commission model that determines how you earn from your referred clients. This guide explains the available commission models, helping you select the one that best suits your business strategy.

Important: New partners need to choose between two primary commission options:
  1. Turnover commission (applies to both CFD and derivatives products), or
  2. Revenue share (applies to derivatives products only)

Additionally, CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) is available for partners referring EU clients, and will soon be available worldwide.

Commission Model Overview

Turnover Commission

Earn a percentage of the trading volume (turnover) generated by your referred clients, regardless of whether they win or lose.

Best for: Partners whose clients trade CFDs or have high trading volumes.

Learn more →

Revenue Share

Earn up to 45% of the revenue Deriv generates from your referred clients' trading activity on derivatives products.

Best for: Partners whose clients primarily trade derivatives products.

Learn more →

CPA (EU Only)

Earn a one-time payment for each qualified client you refer from EU countries, with no ongoing commission.

Best for: Partners focusing on EU markets or preferring one-time payments.

Understanding Turnover Commission

Turnover commission is calculated as a percentage of the total trading volume your referred clients generate, regardless of whether they win or lose on their trades.

Key Features:

  • Applicable to both CFD and derivatives trading - Earn from a wider range of products
  • Based on trading volume, not profitability - Your earnings are more predictable
  • Commission rates vary by product type - Different rates apply to different trading instruments

Example of Turnover Commission Calculation:

If your client trades $100,000 volume of CFDs in a month, and your commission rate is 0.5 pips:

$100,000 × 0.5 pips = $50 commission

For more details and exact rates, visit our Turnover Commission page.

Understanding Revenue Share

Revenue share gives you a percentage of the revenue Deriv earns from your referred clients' trading activity on derivatives products.

Key Features:

  • Applicable to derivatives products only - Not available for CFD trading
  • Higher percentage rates - Up to 45% of company revenue
  • Tiered structure based on performance - Higher volumes can lead to higher percentages

Example of Revenue Share Calculation:

If Deriv earns $2,000 in revenue from your clients' derivatives trading in a month, and your revenue share percentage is 30%:

$2,000 × 30% = $600 commission

For more details on rates and tiers, visit our Revenue Share page.

Understanding CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)

CPA offers a one-time payment for each qualified client you refer, with no ongoing commission. Currently, this model is only available for EU clients but will be expanded globally soon.

Key Features:

  • One-time payment - Earn a fixed amount per qualified client
  • Currently only for EU clients - Will expand to other regions soon
  • Qualification criteria must be met - Clients need to make a minimum deposit and trading activity

Example of CPA Calculation:

If you refer 10 clients who each meet the qualification criteria, and the CPA rate is €200 per client:

10 clients × €200 = €2,000 commission

For more details on qualification criteria and rates, visit our CPA Model page.

How to Choose the Right Commission Model

Decision Guide

1
Consider your target audience: What products are your clients most likely to trade?
  • If they'll primarily trade CFDs, consider Turnover Commission
  • If they'll primarily trade derivatives, either model could work
2
Evaluate trading patterns: How will your clients likely trade?
  • High volume traders may generate more with Turnover Commission
  • Clients who trade derivatives frequently might generate more with Revenue Share
3
Consider predictability vs. potential: What's more important to you?
  • Turnover Commission typically offers more predictable, consistent earnings
  • Revenue Share can offer higher potential earnings but may fluctuate more
4
Geographic focus: Where are your clients located?
  • If focusing primarily on EU clients, CPA might be worth considering
  • For global audiences, choose between Turnover Commission and Revenue Share

Partner Strategies and Commission Models

Your choice of commission model should align with your business strategy and strengths. Here are some common approaches to consider:

Digital Marketing Focus

If you're promoting Deriv through websites, blogs, social media, or other online channels:

  • Revenue Share may work well if your audience trades derivatives
  • Turnover Commission is effective if your audience trades CFDs

Direct Client Focus

If you're directly referring traders and maintaining personal relationships:

  • Turnover Commission provides consistent earnings regardless of client profitability
  • Revenue Share can yield higher returns with active derivatives traders

Technical Solution Focus

If you're developing trading tools or applications using the Deriv API:

  • Choose based on the products your solution supports
  • If supporting multiple products, Turnover Commission offers broader coverage
Remember: You're not limited to a single strategy. Many successful partners combine different approaches based on their strengths and target markets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I change my commission model after signing up?

Yes, you can request to change your commission model once every 30 days. Changes must be requested through your partner account manager and will take effect at the beginning of the next commission period.

Can I use different commission models for different markets?

No, currently you can only select one commission model for all your referred clients. However, you can request to change your model if you find it's not optimal for your business strategy.

How does the payment schedule work?

Commission payments are processed monthly, with payments typically made within the first 15 days of the month following the earning period. For more details, visit our Payment Schedule page.

What is the partner tier system and how does it affect my commission?

The partner tier system rewards partners who refer more active clients with higher commission rates or additional benefits. For more information, visit our Partner Tier System page.

Can I earn commission from sub-partners?

Yes, qualified partners can participate in our sub-partner program, earning a percentage of their sub-partners' commission. This is typically available to partners who have demonstrated success and met certain performance criteria.

How does my promotion strategy affect my commission model choice?

Your promotion strategy and commission model should complement each other. If you're focusing on CFD traders, Turnover Commission will likely be more profitable. If you're primarily promoting derivatives products, Revenue Share might yield better results. The key is aligning your model with your target audience's trading preferences.
